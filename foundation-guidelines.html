Typography

Typography is a critical foundation in design. A well-chosen typeface makes your brand instantly recognizable and sets the entire tone and mood of your design.

Why Typography Matters

  • Brand Recognition: A specific font makes your brand memorable and distinguishable. Generic typography goes unnoticed.
  • Setting the Mood: Typography establishes the "vibe" of your design. A nature-based shop might use organic, flowing fonts, while a tech startup uses robotic, professional typefaces.
  • Visual Hierarchy & Diversity: Using multiple fonts breaks up monotony and helps users understand content structure. Without variation, designs feel flat and mushed together.

Best Practices

Do's

  • Use fonts to set the "vibe"
  • Break up text with different fonts
  • Make typography memorable and unique
  • Keep fonts legible and scalable
  • Use typography to establish hierarchy

Don'ts

  • Use unreadable or illegible fonts
  • Use the same font for entire designs
  • Use uniform font sizes everywhere
  • Use too many fonts (aim for 2-3)
  • Ignore accessibility in font choices
